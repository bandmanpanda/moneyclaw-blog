What Is Hyaluronic Acid?

Hyaluronic acid (HA) is a naturally occurring glycosaminoglycan ‚Äî a sugar molecule ‚Äî found throughout your body. About 50% of your body‚Äôs total HA is in your skin, where it plays a crucial role in retaining moisture and maintaining plumpness.

How It Actually Works

HA is a humectant ‚Äî it draws water from the environment and deeper layers of skin to the surface. One molecule of hyaluronic acid can hold up to 1,000 times its weight in water (this claim is technically true but slightly misleading in practice ‚Äî more on that later).

Molecular Weight Matters

Not all hyaluronic acid is created equal. HA comes in different molecular weights, and each works differently:

High molecular weight HA (>1000 kDa)

  • Sits on the skin surface
  • Creates a moisture-retaining film
  • Immediate plumping effect
  • Cannot penetrate the skin barrier

Low molecular weight HA (50-1000 kDa)

  • Penetrates into the upper layers of skin (epidermis)
  • Hydrates from within
  • Longer-lasting effects
  • Can sometimes cause irritation in very sensitive skin

Nano/Ultra-low molecular weight HA (<50 kDa)

  • Penetrates deeper into skin
  • May stimulate the skin‚Äôs own HA production
  • Some studies suggest anti-inflammatory benefits
  • Controversial ‚Äî some research suggests it may trigger inflammatory responses at very low molecular weights

Yes, hyaluronic acid can technically hold 1,000 times its weight in water in laboratory conditions. But on your face, in the real world, it doesn‚Äôt work quite that dramatically. In a dry environment (arid climate, heated indoor air), HA can actually pull moisture from deeper layers of your skin to the surface, where it evaporates. How to Use Hyaluronic Acid Correctly

This is where most people go wrong:

Step 1: Apply to Damp Skin

HA needs water to work. Apply immediately after cleansing while your skin is still damp, or spritz your face with water/toner first. Never apply to dry skin.

Step 2: Use the Right Amount

2-3 drops of serum, or a thin layer if using a toner format. More isn‚Äôt better ‚Äî excess product just sits on the surface.

Step 3: Seal It In

Apply moisturizer within 60 seconds of your HA product. This traps the moisture and prevents it from evaporating. In dry climates, use an occlusive moisturizer containing petrolatum, dimethicone, or shea butter.

Step 4: Use Twice Daily

HA is gentle enough for morning and evening use. No need to build tolerance.

Hyaluronic Acid vs. Other Hydrating Ingredients
