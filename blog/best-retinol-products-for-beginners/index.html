Retinol 101: What You Need to Know

What Is Retinol?

Retinol is a derivative of vitamin A. When applied to skin, it converts to retinoic acid ‚Äî the active form that speeds up cell turnover, stimulates collagen production, and regulates pigmentation. Prescription tretinoin is already retinoic acid; retinol requires conversion, which makes it gentler but slower-acting.

The Retinoid Hierarchy (Weakest to Strongest)

  1. Retinyl palmitate ‚Äî very gentle, minimal results
  2. Retinol ‚Äî the sweet spot for beginners, OTC available
  3. Retinal (retinaldehyde) ‚Äî one conversion step from retinoic acid, faster results
  4. Adapalene (Differin) ‚Äî OTC retinoid, great for acne
  5. Tretinoin ‚Äî prescription, strongest, fastest results

For beginners, retinol at 0.2-0.5% concentration is the ideal starting point.

What Retinol Actually Does

  • Accelerates cell turnover (smoother texture, fewer clogged pores)
  • Stimulates collagen and elastin production (firmer, plumper skin)
  • Inhibits melanin production (fades dark spots)
  • Normalizes oil production (helps with acne)
  • Thickens the dermis over time (stronger, more resilient skin)

Results are cumulative and take 3-6 months to become clearly visible. This is a long game.

How to Start Retinol: The Beginner Protocol

Week 1-2: Introduction Phase

  • Apply retinol once per week, at night
  • Use a pea-sized amount for your entire face
  • Apply to dry skin (wet skin increases penetration and irritation)
  • Wait 20 minutes after cleansing before applying
  • Follow with a rich moisturizer

Week 3-4: Building Phase

  • Increase to twice per week
  • Monitor for excessive dryness, peeling, or redness
  • If irritated, stay at once per week for another 2 weeks

Week 5-8: Establishing Phase

  • Increase to every other night
  • Your skin should be adjusting ‚Äî mild dryness is normal, painful peeling is not

Week 9+: Maintenance

  • Use every night or every other night based on tolerance
  • Some people‚Äôs skin never tolerates nightly use, and that‚Äôs perfectly fine
